Affordability · Housing Economics
Where does rent consume the largest share of salary — and is it worsening?
Rent-to-salary ratio is the single most critical affordability measure for workers, expats and policy-makers. When rent exceeds 30–40% of income, households face "cost burden", cutting into food, healthcare and savings. This analysis reveals which cities are unaffordable today, and which are trending worse — informing decisions for relocating professionals, HR teams setting compensation, and housing policy advocates.

Wellbeing · Urban Planning
Which cities offer the best Quality of Life relative to their cost burden?
High cost of living is tolerable when quality of life is also high — but many cities charge a premium without the payoff. Plotting Quality of Life Index against Rent Cost % reveals four quadrants: sweet spots (high QoL, low cost), poverty traps (low QoL, high cost), premium cities worth it, and bargains. This is essential for talent mobility decisions, expatriate planning, and government competitiveness reports.

Public Health · Liveability
How do Safety, Healthcare and Pollution trade off across regions — and which cities are genuinely liveable?
Liveability goes beyond cost — a cheap city with high pollution, poor healthcare and low safety is a false bargain. This radar-style analysis scores cities on three pillars of liveability to reveal which regions consistently excel, which have hidden risks, and how city-level performance compares within the same country. Critical for international health organisations, insurance risk modelling, and individuals weighing where to live and raise families.
